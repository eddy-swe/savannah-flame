Our Founding Story

From Dakar to Oslo

✦

In 2009, Chef Amara Diallo arrived in Oslo with two suitcases and a battered notebook filled with recipes transcribed by hand from her grandmother's kitchen in Dakar. She had worked in fine dining kitchens from Nairobi to Paris, but she knew that what the world was missing wasn't more French technique — it was the soulful, complex, deeply human cuisine of Africa.

Savannah Flame opened in a small 32-seat space in Grünerløkka. Within a year, it had a waiting list. Within three years, it had been named Oslo's Best New Restaurant. Today, the restaurant seats 120 guests across two dining rooms and a private event space, but the ethos remains exactly as Amara wrote it in that notebook on the flight from Dakar:

"Every guest should leave not just well-fed, but changed — connected to a continent, a culture, and a story they didn't know before they sat down."

We cook with fire, with spice, and with love. We source from the best organic farms in Norway and the most trusted importers of African ingredients in Europe. We employ chefs who have trained in their home regions and who bring a genuine piece of their culture to every dish.

Mission & Values

The principles that guide every decision we make, from sourcing ingredients to designing menus.

01
🌍

Authenticity Above All

We never trade cultural integrity for convenience. Our recipes are researched, tested with community elders, and prepared with techniques that honour their origins.

We partner exclusively with certified organic Norwegian producers and Fair Trade African importers. Sustainability is not a marketing word for us — it's a daily commitment.

We refresh our menu seasonally, incorporating new ingredients, new regions, and new stories. Tradition and innovation are not opposites — they are partners.

Our Journey

15 Years of Excellence

2009

The Beginning

Savannah Flame opens its doors in Grünerløkka with 32 seats. Full house on opening night; waiting list within 3 months.

2011

Best New Restaurant

Named Oslo's Best New Restaurant by Matmagasinet. Chef Amara featured in Time Out Scandinavia's top 10 chefs.

2014

Expansion

We move to our current location — a converted 19th-century warehouse — expanding to 120 seats and adding the Kalahari private dining room.

2017

Education Fund Launch

The Savannah Education Fund is established. First cohort of 4 students from Ghana, Kenya, and Senegal sponsored through culinary training.

2020

Resilience & Reinvention

During the pandemic, we launched Savannah at Home — a nationwide delivery of preparation kits that reached 40+ cities across Norway.

2024

Nordic Gastronomy Award

Awarded the Nordic Gastronomy Prize for Cultural Contribution — recognising our role in establishing African cuisine as a serious fine dining category.

Head Chef & Founder

Meet Our Chef

Chef Amara Diallo

✦

Born in Dakar, Senegal, Amara learned to cook at her grandmother's side before earning her formal training at the Institut Paul Bocuse in Lyon. She went on to work at Michelin-starred restaurants in Paris, Nairobi, and Cape Town before founding Savannah Flame at the age of 31.

Her philosophy is simple: treat every ingredient like a living story. Her approach has won her recognition across Europe, but she'll tell you her greatest honour was having her grandmother fly to Oslo for the restaurant's 10th anniversary dinner and pronounce the jollof rice "acceptable."
